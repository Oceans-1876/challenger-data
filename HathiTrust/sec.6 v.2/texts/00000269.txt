Depth, 2700 fathoms; deposit, Radiolarian Ooze, containing only a trace of
carbonate of lime (see Murray and Renard, Deep-Sea Deposits Chall. Exp.).
At 5,20 A.M. stopped and sounded in 2700 fathoms; only a small quantity of deposit
came up in the sounding-tube. Obtained serial temperatures down to 1500 fathoms,
and also specimens of water. The carbonic acid was determined in bottom water, and
amounted to 40.5 milligrammes per litre. At 1 P.m. lowered four tow-nets attached a
short distance from each other to the dredging-rope, and veered 2000 fathoms. Sent
dingey away for surface collecting and to test the transparency of the water. At 2 P.M.
commenced heaving in tow-nets which came up all foul at 3.30 P.M.
At 4 P.M.
proceeded under steam and sail. Several boobies and gannets were seen.
Venus Point, Tahiti, distant at noon, 1620 miles. Made good 97 miles. Amount
of current 19 miles, direction S. 34° E.
