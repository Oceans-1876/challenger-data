THE VOYAGE OF H.M.S. CHALLENGER.
STATION 264.
Depth, 3000 fathoms; deposit, Red Clay, containing only a trace of carbonate of
lime (see Murray and Renard, Deep-Sea Deposits Chall. Exp.).
.
At 4.10 A. M. shortened and furled sails. Got up steam. At 5 A.M. put trawl over,
with two tow-nets attached and a tow-net at the weights, and veered 3000 fathoms.
At 6.30 A.M. brought ship to wind, and at 7 A.M. sounded in 3000 fathoms. Veered
trawl-rope to 3700 fathoms. Obtained serial temperatures down to 1500 fathoms.
At 1 P.M. commenced heaving in trawl, which came up much torn at 4.30 P.M., but
containing a few manganese nodules and specimens. At 6 P.m. made all plain sail. A
few gannets were seen.
Venus Point, Tahiti, distant at noon, 1920 miles. Made good 84 miles. Amount
of current 33 miles, direction S. 58° W.
ANIMALS FROM
TRAWL.
The following species is recorded from the trawl at this Station :-
MONAXONIDA (Ridley and Dendy, Zool. pt. 59).
Cladorhiza longipinna, n.sp. One specimen ; obtained at no other locality.
In addition, the Station-Book records a fragment of Euplectella and a large specimen
of Stephanoscyphus attached to manganese nodule.
