THE VOYAGE OF H.M.S. CHALLENGER.
STATION 300.
At 4.20 A.M. observed the island of Juan Fernandez in the west. At 4.30 A.M.
shortened and furled sails, and got up steam. At 5.15 A.M. put trawl over and veered
2700 fathoms. At 6.30 A.m. brought ship to wind and sounded in 1375 fathoms.
Obtained serial temperatures down to bottom. The carbonic acid was determined in
water from 50 fathoms and the bottom, and amounted respectively to 50:4 and
49:3 milligrammes per litre. At 1 P.M. commenced heaving in trawl, which came up at
4.25 P.M. with numerous specimens and a considerable quantity of ooze, in which were
several small basaltic pebbles with a slight coating of manganese, and flat pieces of
hardened tufa of a red colour with a thick coating of manganese. At 4.30 P.M. proceeded
under steam and sail. Juan Fernandez in sight the whole afternoon. Diomedea exulans,
Diomedea melanophrys (?), Procellaria maxima, mutton birds, petrels, Prion turtur, and
Thalassidroma were seen.
Juan Fernandez (East Pt.) distant at noon, 23 miles. Made good 74 miles.

Station :
HEXACTINELLIDA (Schulze, Zool. pt. 53).
Hyalonema poculum, n.sp. One specimen ; obtained at no other locality.
ACTINIARIA (Hertwig, Zool. pt. 15).
Corallimorphus profundus, Moseley, n.g., n.sp. One specimen ; obtained also at
Station 293.
Paractis excavata, n.sp. One specimen ; obtained at no other locality.
Ophiodiscus sulcatus, n.g., n.sp. One specimen; obtained at no other locality.
